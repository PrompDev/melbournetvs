# Melbourne TVs market context and launch recommendations

Research date: 19 July 2026. Advertised prices are public signals, not a verified market average or a wholesale installer rate card.

## Executive recommendation

Launch Melbourne TVs as a photo-first, transparently quoted local booking service. The strongest position is the middle of the current market: easier and more accountable than a low-price handyman, but simpler and clearer than an electrical/AV bundle.

The initial promise should be:

- a firm written price after the customer sends the TV size/model, wall photo and postcode;
- a defined arrival window, clear inclusions, clean-up and a completed-install photo;
- the correct technician for the work: mounting specialist for an ordinary bracket install, licensed electrical contractor for fixed electrical work, and registered cabler for regulated data/communications cabling;
- a single, consistent workmanship promise backed by the operator, even when a subcontractor performs the job.

Do **not** claim “same day”, “Melbourne-wide”, “licensed installers”, a review score, or a long warranty until the roster and evidence support each claim. Keep Brisbane and Melbourne lead data, phone attribution, reviews, pricing and service coverage separate.

### Sensible launch offer to test

Use three simple quote categories, but keep the website price as “from” until the installation roster is proven:

1. **Mount only** — customer-supplied compatible bracket, level and secure installation, connection/testing and clean-up. Test a public starting band of **$169–$199**.
2. **Clean setup** — mounting plus an appropriate slim bracket and an agreed cable-concealment method. Test **$249–$329**, with the exact price driven by TV size and wall construction.
3. **Large, specialty or licensed-work quote** — 85-inch-plus TVs, LG Gallery/Samsung Frame, full-motion brackets, fireplaces, masonry, soundbar, new power/data/antenna points or difficult access. Quote from photos and send a two-person team where the safe handling plan requires it.

Useful add-on bands to test are **$75–$100** for concealment where feasible and **$89–$119** for a soundbar. Do not make a new power point an anonymous “add-on”: identify the licensed electrical contractor and include the required electrical certificate in the job record.

## Melbourne customer and property context

Greater Melbourne is not an apartment-only market. The [2021 ABS Greater Melbourne QuickStats](https://www.abs.gov.au/census/find-census-data/quickstats/2021/2GMEL) counted 1,781,346 occupied private dwellings: 67.8% separate houses, 16.2% semi-detached/terrace/townhouses and 15.6% flats or apartments. It also recorded 30.2% of occupied dwellings as rented. The operational implication is that both suburban houses and rental/apartment constraints deserve first-class booking flows.

The quote form should capture:

- postcode/suburb, TV brand/model/size and approximate weight;
- wide and close wall photos, current power/antenna/data location, and whether a cavity is accessible;
- wall type if known: plaster/timber stud, brick, concrete, tile or uncertain;
- customer-supplied bracket versus fixed, tilt or full-motion bracket required;
- soundbar, console, streaming box, antenna, Ethernet and HDMI requirements;
- stairs, lift/loading access, parking, concierge/contractor rules and preferred arrival window;
- owner or renter, heritage/asbestos concern, and whether written permission is already available;
- whether an existing TV must be removed, transported or disposed of.

### Rental, apartment and older-home issues

[Consumer Affairs Victoria](https://www.consumer.vic.gov.au/housing/renting/repairs-alterations-safety-and-pets/renters-making-changes-to-the-property) says renters may install screws for wall mounts without permission on surfaces other than exposed brick or concrete when the property is not on the Victorian Heritage Register. Exposed brick/concrete changes fall into a category where permission generally cannot be unreasonably refused, but the renter should ask. Renters may still have to restore the property or pay reasonable restoration costs at the end of the agreement. The site should explain this carefully, not give a blanket “no permission needed” answer.

For apartments and units, [Consumer Affairs Victoria explains](https://www.consumer.vic.gov.au/housing/owners-corporations/buying-into-an-owners-corporation/what-is-an-owners-corporation) that walls, lifts, foyers and other areas can be common property and that owners-corporation rules apply to owners and tenants. Ask the customer to confirm contractor access, parking/lift rules and whether the intended wall is inside their lot before the appointment.

Consumer Affairs Victoria also warns not to drill suspected asbestos-containing material and suggests considering an asbestos assessment for homes built before 1990. An installer should stop, photograph and escalate when wall material is uncertain; “we mount every wall” is an unsafe promise.

### Compliance boundary worth making visible

Victoria's [Electrical Safety Exemptions Order](https://www.energysafe.vic.gov.au/sites/default/files/2022-12/Electricity-Safety-Exemptions-Order-2020.pdf) expressly gives ordinary TV bracket mounting and connection with the manufactured plug/appliance connector as an example of exempt plug-in-appliance work. Changing or adding a power point is electrical work; [Energy Safe Victoria](https://www.energysafe.vic.gov.au/electrical-ddiy-dont-do-it-yourself) says power-point work must be done by a licensed electrician, and a Certificate of Electrical Safety is required for electrical installation work.

For data/communications cabling, the [ACMA](https://www.acma.gov.au/find-registered-cabler) says a registered cabler must perform or directly supervise the work and provide a signed compliance statement. The website and call script should never blur simple loose HDMI/TV leads with regulated in-wall customer cabling.

## Services customers now expect

The competitor set consistently sells more than drilling four holes. Melbourne TVs should define whether each of these is included, available as an add-on, or out of scope:

- unbox, inspect and place the TV at a comfortable agreed height;
- supply/check fixed, tilt and full-motion brackets against TV weight and VESA pattern;
- connect and test plug-in devices, tune channels, basic Wi-Fi/smart-TV setup and a short handover;
- concealed cables where the wall permits, or neat surface conduit where it does not;
- soundbar mounting, second-TV pricing and removal of an existing TV;
- clean-up and before/after photographs;
- retailer pickup/delivery only if insurance and handling responsibility are explicit;
- licensed power-point work and registered data/antenna work through clearly identified partners.

The clean differentiator is not a longer list. It is a quote that says exactly what will happen to the cables on **this wall**, who will do regulated work, and what would change the price.

## Trust and conversion signals to build

Prioritise verifiable proof in this order:

1. Real Melbourne business identity, ABN/entity details, local phone routing, privacy notice and truthful service area.
2. Named/photographed installers, current public-liability certificate, and recorded licence/registration details for the work each person performs.
3. Written quote before work starts, itemised invoice, payment options and a visible “no surprise changes without approval” rule.
4. A two-person handling rule for TVs or access conditions that require it.
5. Job checklist: wall/fixing assessment, bracket capacity, level/alignment, pull/safety check, device test, clean-up and completion photo.
6. Consented Melbourne before/after jobs labelled with the real suburb, wall type, TV model/size and work completed.
7. Reviews requested only after genuine Melbourne jobs and linked to the matching Melbourne business profile. Never import Brisbane review claims.
8. A consistent arrival/rescheduling process and one accountable contact for callbacks.

## Warranty, cancellation and callback policy

The [ACCC's consumer-guarantee guidance](https://www.accc.gov.au/consumers/buying-products-and-services/consumer-rights-and-guarantees) requires services to be delivered with due care and skill, fit for a disclosed purpose and within a reasonable time where no time is agreed. Any Melbourne TVs workmanship warranty is an extra promise and cannot replace those rights.

Recommended policy design:

- choose **one** workmanship period and use it everywhere; do not repeat Brisbane's lifetime/five-year/12-month inconsistency;
- state exactly what it covers: installer-supplied fixings and mounting workmanship, plus the process for a free inspection/remedy;
- separately identify manufacturer warranty on supplied brackets and reasonable exclusions such as TV faults, undisclosed wall defects, later third-party changes or an incompatible customer-supplied bracket;
- keep the business accountable for subcontractor workmanship instead of telling the customer to chase the installer;
- record wall photos, fixings, bracket model/capacity and completion acceptance so callbacks can be resolved fairly;
- preserve all statutory rights and never publish “no refunds”. [Consumer Affairs Victoria](https://www.consumer.vic.gov.au/consumers-and-businesses/products-and-services/problem-with-a-service/cancelling-a-contract-for-services) confirms that a consumer can cancel and seek the relevant remedy when a service fails a consumer guarantee.

For customer change-of-mind cancellations, disclose the rule before booking. A practical launch policy is free rescheduling with reasonable notice, no deposit for routine jobs, and a capped late-cancellation/callout fee only where it reflects reasonable costs actually incurred. If Melbourne TVs cancels, return any prepayment promptly and offer a new time. Avoid punitive, automatic or hidden fees.

## Melbourne-specific FAQ set

Write real answers to these before launch:

1. How much does TV wall mounting cost in Melbourne, and what can change the quote?
2. Can I get a firm price from photos before the installer arrives?
3. Do you mount on plaster/studs, brick, concrete and tiled walls?
4. Can cables be hidden in my wall, and what happens if there is no usable cavity?
5. Do I need a new power point behind the TV, and who issues the electrical certificate?
6. Can you install Ethernet, antenna or other in-wall communications cabling?
7. Can you mount Samsung Frame, LG Gallery/OLED, full-motion and 85-inch-plus TVs?
8. Can I supply my own bracket? What compatibility details do you need?
9. Can you mount a TV in a rental? What written permission or restoration may be needed?
10. Do you work in CBD/high-rise apartments, and what access/parking information is required?
11. What happens if the installer finds suspected asbestos or an unsuitable wall?
12. Which suburbs are covered, what are the real response windows, and is there a travel charge?
13. What does the workmanship warranty cover, and who handles a callback?
14. What is the rescheduling/cancellation policy?

## Avoiding a thin Brisbane clone

Re-use the tested technical foundation, components, forms and visual system. Do not mass-publish the Brisbane copy with “Brisbane” swapped for “Melbourne”. Google's current [spam policies](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/essentials/spam-policies) identify substantially similar regional pages and scaled, low-value pages as doorway/scaled-content abuse; its [people-first guidance](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/creating-helpful-content) asks for first-hand expertise, original value and a satisfying answer for the intended audience.

Practical publishing rules:

- launch with the homepage, core service/pricing/quote pages, legal pages and a small number of genuine service-zone hubs;
- publish a suburb page only when service capacity, arrival/travel rules and the quote path for that suburb are real;
- make each local page useful with actual job evidence: TV/wall type, access issue, cable solution, price factors, local response window and consented photos;
- do not fabricate landmarks, house types, “local technician” claims, suburb reviews, recent jobs or an office address;
- use one clear hierarchy (Melbourne → zone → supported suburbs), not hundreds of near-identical pages that all funnel to the same generic page;
- self-canonicalise real unique pages and keep unpublished/template-only pages out of the sitemap and index;
- replace all Brisbane-specific phone, forms, spreadsheet IDs, review counts, suburb data, schema, legal entity details, pricing and warranty text;
- add a lightweight content feedback loop: installer completion notes feed real FAQs and case studies after every job.

## Launch gates

Before paid traffic:

- at least one vetted installer in each advertised zone, with a backup path and explicit licensed-work coverage;
- signed rate card, service areas, availability, insurance, warranty/callback and no-poach/data-use terms;
- final customer prices tested against net fulfilment cost and callback allowance;
- friend/operator trained on the photo-first qualification script and what cannot be quoted blindly;
- separate Melbourne phone, lead sheet, calendar access and campaign attribution working end to end;
- first real install completed with customer consent, photos, invoice and a callback test;
- every public claim matched to current evidence.

The winning early version is a small, accurate Melbourne operation with strong proof. Expand suburb coverage and SEO pages after fulfilment data shows where Melbourne TVs can genuinely deliver.
