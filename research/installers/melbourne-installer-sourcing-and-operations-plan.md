# Melbourne TVs installer sourcing and launch operations plan

Research date: 19 July 2026 (AEST)

Status: research shortlist only — nobody below has been contacted, agreed to subcontract, or been approved.

## Executive recommendation

Launch as a **managed referral service** first: Melbourne TVs answers the call, qualifies the job, obtains the customer's express agreement to pass the job details to a local installer, and dispatches it. The attending installer confirms the final scope, contracts with the customer and invoices the customer. Say this clearly on the quote form and booking confirmation.

Do not mix that with a prime-contractor model. If Melbourne TVs sets the binding final price, collects the customer's money or promises the workmanship in its own name, it should first obtain legal/accounting advice and insurance for the larger obligations it is taking on. Australian Consumer Law requires services to be provided with due care and skill, fit for a stated purpose and within a reasonable time; a subcontract does not make those promises disappear for the business that sold the service ([ACCC](https://www.accc.gov.au/consumers/buying-products-and-services/consumer-rights-and-guarantees)).

The launch gate should be:

1. one approved primary and one approved backup in each active zone;
2. at least one REC-backed electrical escalation partner available for every zone;
3. current insurance and registration evidence on file, independently checked rather than accepted from a website;
4. signed privacy, no-poach, pricing, safety and warranty terms;
5. two paid trial jobs per installer, including one large-TV or non-plaster job, pass quality review;
6. no paid ads pointed at a zone until its primary and backup pass the gate.

## Hard vetting gates and scorecard

Reject or pause a candidate if any gate fails; do not average a missing legal/safety item into a good marketing score.

### Gate documents

- legal entity name, ABN and trading name, checked in ABN Lookup;
- photo ID and names of every technician who may enter a home;
- current public-liability certificate of currency (target $20m), with business activities covering mounting/AV/electrical work and subcontracted work where relevant;
- workers compensation evidence if the contractor employs staff, and vehicle/tools cover as relevant;
- REC and individual electrical licence evidence for power-point or fixed-wiring work;
- current cabler registration plus the correct competencies for regulated telecommunications/data work;
- written two-person lifting rule and high-risk/unsafe-wall stop-work procedure;
- signed contractor agreement and rate card;
- two satisfactory references or equivalent recent-work evidence;
- two paid trial jobs passing the completion checklist.

Victoria's exemptions order gives the example that a person may mount a manufacturer-supplied plug-and-cord TV and plug it into a suitable socket ([Electricity Safety Exemptions Order 2020](https://www.esv.vic.gov.au/sites/default/files/2022-12/Electricity-Safety-Exemptions-Order-2020.pdf)). That is not permission to install or relocate fixed power. ESV guidance says a Certificate of Electrical Safety is required for electrical installation work ([ESV COES fundamentals](https://www.esv.vic.gov.au/sites/default/files/2022-12/COES_Fundamentals_Aug2021.pdf)). ACMA says regulated customer cabling must be performed or supervised by a properly registered cabler and certified after the work ([Cabling Provider Rules](https://www.acma.gov.au/cabling-provider-rules), [cabling advice forms](https://www.acma.gov.au/cabling-advice-forms)).

## Rate-card interview

Ask every candidate for GST-inclusive wholesale and customer-facing prices, with inclusions/exclusions, for:

- labour-only mounting: up to 55, 56–65, 66–75, 76–85, 86–98, 99+ inches;
- supplied fixed, tilt and full-motion brackets by weight and VESA rating;
- plaster/timber stud, steel stud, brick, concrete, stone, tiled and above-fireplace work;
- Samsung Frame / LG Gallery / flush-mount jobs;
- cable concealment using brush plates/conduit, HDMI/optical/coax runs and device setup;
- soundbar, shelf, gaming-console, aerial point and data point add-ons;
- a new or relocated power point completed by a licensed electrician, including COES;
- second technician/large-screen lift, staircase or restricted-access surcharge;
- apartment loading/parking, paid parking and body-corporate induction;
- outer-zone travel bands, after-hours/weekend work and minimum callout;
- customer-supplied bracket assessment and unusable/missing-hardware fee;
- no-access, same-day cancellation, reschedule and quote-only attendance;
- warranty return, non-warranty return and disputed-cause inspection;
- payment timing, invoice evidence, materials markup and price-review frequency.

Capacity questions:

1. Which postcodes can you reliably reach Monday–Friday and Saturday?
2. How many standard jobs and 85+ inch two-person jobs can you accept weekly?
3. What assignment notice is needed and what percentage can be same-day/next-day?
4. Who answers if the assigned technician is sick or delayed?
5. Which work must be declined or referred to an electrician/cabler?
6. Can you upload the full completion pack before leaving the property?
7. Will you work under a disclosed “Melbourne TVs arranged this job; your installation contract is with [Installer]” script?

## Dispatch workflow

1. **Lead intake:** Melbourne TVs records name, phone, suburb, TV size/model, bracket, wall type, cable/power needs, preferred time, access/parking and photos. Store only what is needed.
2. **Customer disclosure and consent:** explain who will receive the details and that the accepted installer is the contracting/invoicing party. Obtain explicit consent before sharing the address or contact details.
3. **Remote safety/scope triage:** flag 85+ inch, stone/tile, fireplace, steel studs, outdoor/alfresco, new power/data, very high mount, stairs and apartments.
4. **Zone match:** offer a privacy-minimised card (suburb, scope, time, TV size; no name/address) to the primary installer. Five-minute response target, then backup. Do not blast full customer data to a group.
5. **Acceptance:** after a named installer accepts, release the minimum required customer details and log exactly who received them.
6. **Final scope and price:** installer confirms wall/access and any permitted variation before travel or work. The booking message names the installer/business and states who invoices.
7. **Arrival control:** 30-minute ETA text, branded ID, no substitute technician without approval, and check-in timestamp.
8. **Before photos and stop-work check:** wall, services, bracket rating, TV condition/serial if appropriate, position approval and hazards. Do not proceed when wall structure, heat, electrical or cabling compliance is uncertain.
9. **Installation:** follow mount manufacturer limits and installer safe-work process. Any electrical or regulated cabling component goes only to the correctly credentialed person.
10. **Completion pack:** before/after photos, bracket/model, wall/fixing description, work completed, variation approval, customer sign-off, installer invoice, warranty, COES/TCA statement when applicable, and any damage/incident note.
11. **Customer QA:** automated same-day check plus human follow-up for low scores or issues. Record contacted, quoted, booked, completed, revenue/referral fee, installer, warranty and lost reason in the Melbourne sheet.
12. **Payment and score update:** release contractor/referral settlement only after the complete pack; update on-time, complaint, rework and acceptance-rate metrics monthly.

## Minimum contractor terms

Have an Australian lawyer adapt the agreement before launch. Operationally it should cover:

- **Model and customer disclosure:** installer is an independent business and the customer-facing contracting party under the recommended referral model; no authority to bind Melbourne TVs or misrepresent employment/partnership.
- **Scope and licensing:** installer only accepts work it is competent, registered and insured to perform; no fixed electrical work or regulated cabling outside licence/registration limits; immediate notice of lapse, suspension, incident or claim.
- **Prices and variations:** rate card is fixed for an agreed period; no variation or upsell without written customer approval and Melbourne TVs notification; no cash side deals.
- **No-poach / lead ownership:** no direct marketing, diversion or off-platform follow-on work for referred customers for 12 months, excluding documented pre-existing customers; all customer requests are reported back. The clause should protect legitimate customer relationships without becoming an unreasonable restraint.
- **Privacy and security:** use customer data only to quote, attend and warranty the assigned job; no list-building or marketing; least-access dispatch; secure devices; no sharing with another technician without approval; report a suspected breach immediately (target within 24 hours); return/delete data after the required warranty/legal-retention period.
- **Customer communications:** accurate business identity, ETA, polite home conduct, no discriminatory/unsafe behaviour, clean-up and no photography beyond job evidence without consent.
- **Safety:** right and duty to stop; wall/service checks; manufacturer limits; two-person lift threshold; ladder/working-at-height, dust and manual-handling rules; incident reporting and preservation of evidence. WorkSafe notes that businesses can retain OHS duties for contractors in matters they control ([WorkSafe Victoria](https://www.worksafe.vic.gov.au/general-duties-relating-health-and-safety)).
- **Completion evidence:** required photos and certificates, customer sign-off, itemised materials and serial/bracket details.
- **Warranty and rework:** workmanship warranty owner, response within one business day, urgent make-safe immediately, inspection/repair window, responsibility allocation and Melbourne TVs' right to arrange rectification and recover a proven installer-caused cost.
- **Damage and claims:** immediate notification, no admission/settlement without insurer process, cooperation with evidence and clear excess allocation.
- **Substitution/subcontracting:** no substitute or sub-subcontractor without prior approval and the same documents/terms.
- **Payment:** complete job pack as condition of payment; GST/tax responsibility; dispute process and permitted set-off for agreed rectification.
- **Termination:** immediate suspension for expired insurance/licence, privacy misuse, safety breach, customer diversion or material complaint; data return/deletion at exit.
- **Worker status:** structure the real working relationship consistently with contractor status; calling someone a contractor is not enough, and sham contracting is unlawful ([Fair Work Ombudsman](https://www.fairwork.gov.au/find-help-for/independent-contractors/sham-contracting)).

Even if the business may initially fall under a small-business Privacy Act exemption, run to an APP-style standard. OAIC notes that some small businesses are covered, including businesses trading in personal information, and recommends contracts control contractor handling of personal information ([OAIC small business](https://www.oaic.gov.au/privacy/privacy-guidance-for-organisations-and-government-agencies/organisations/small-business), [OAIC contractor-control guidance](https://www.oaic.gov.au/privacy/australian-privacy-principles/australian-privacy-principles-guidelines/chapter-b-key-concepts)). The website consent should say customer details are shared with the selected installer to quote and perform the requested service — not sold to an unknown list.

## Staged launch

### Stage 0 — controls before leads (2–3 working days)

- finalise the disclosed referral model, privacy notice, booking script and contractor agreement;
- add installer, zone, job status, quote, completed value, referral margin, certificates, complaint and warranty fields to the Melbourne sheet;
- create the five-zone map and postcode/travel bands;
- prepare document-expiry reminders and an approved/paused installer register;
- prepare the quote/photo intake and completion-pack forms.

### Stage 1 — recruit and prove the core ring (week 1)

- seek two candidates per active zone plus the two REC-backed escalation partners;
- document/interview score before a trial;
- run two paid, consented trial jobs per candidate: one standard plaster/timber-stud mount and one harder-but-in-scope job;
- cap each new installer at two live jobs until both are QA-complete;
- publish only suburbs covered by an approved primary and backup.

### Stage 2 — controlled soft launch (week 2)

- open organic enquiries in inner, north, west, east and south-east core suburbs;
- daily cap: no more confirmed jobs than the next two business days of verified installer capacity;
- friend answering calls uses the same qualification and disclosure script;
- review every completion pack and call every early customer;
- pause an installer immediately after a safety, privacy, licence or serious damage concern; pause after two avoidable service failures pending review.

### Stage 3 — paid acquisition (after at least 10 clean completed jobs)

- enable paid ads only for proven postcode clusters;
- start with one or two dense clusters per zone rather than “all Melbourne”;
- report cost per qualified lead, quote, booked job and completed contribution margin — not just form submissions;
- maintain at least 20% spare weekly capacity and a live backup before increasing spend.

### Stage 4 — outer growth corridors

Expand only when travel-band economics and backups are proven: Melton/Rockbank, Wyndham/Manor Lakes, Mickleham/Wallan, Doreen/Whittlesea, Lilydale/Yarra Valley, Clyde/Pakenham/Cardinia and the Mornington Peninsula. Each corridor needs its own surcharge/minimum, primary, backup and licensed escalation path.

## Weekly operating dashboard

Track by installer and zone:

- offered, accepted and declined jobs; median acceptance time;
- lead-to-contact, contact-to-quote, quote-to-book and booked-to-complete;
- on-time arrival and same/next-day capacity;
- average customer price, installer cost/referral fee, contribution margin and travel cost;
- completion-pack compliance and required certificate compliance;
- first-time completion, rework within 30 days, damage incidents and complaints;
- customer score and low-score recovery time;
- insurance/licence expiry, paused status and trial/approved tier;
- customer diversion/no-poach exceptions and privacy incidents.

The operational rule is simple: **do not market a suburb merely because a website says an installer covers it. Market it only after a named primary and backup have signed, proven capacity and passed trial work.**
