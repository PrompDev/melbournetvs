# Melbourne TVs suburb and service-area launch plan

Prepared 19 July 2026 for the Melbourne TVs launch.

## Decision summary

Use one Melbourne brand and nine operational service zones. Do not promise undifferentiated “all Melbourne, same day” coverage while the installer network is still being built.

The source inventory contains **534 official bounded localities** in the 31 Plan Melbourne metropolitan council areas, plus **Beveridge and Wallan** from the metropolitan portion of Mitchell Shire. It is intentionally broader than the dense urban footprint. This gives the business a complete dispatch reference without forcing every rural, industrial or restricted locality to become an indexed landing page.

Recommended rollout:

1. Publish the homepage, service pages, nine zone pages and the 74 Tier 1 suburb pages only after installer capacity is confirmed for those areas.
2. Treat the 276 Tier 2 records as a review-and-release queue. Promote them in batches using real lead, call and installer-availability data.
3. Keep 137 Tier 3 localities in the service-area finder but do not create indexed pages by default.
4. Keep 47 Tier 4 localities as quote-only or restricted-access records. They should not be advertised as normal coverage.

This structure supports the user-facing claim “servicing Melbourne and surrounding suburbs” while keeping booking promises truthful.

## What “all suburbs” means in this dataset

The Victorian Government source calls these places **bounded localities**. The commercial site can call them suburbs and areas, but the data uses the registered locality spelling as its canonical source.

The metropolitan planning regions consist of entire councils except for Mitchell Shire. Planning Victoria says only the southern part of Mitchell within the Urban Growth Boundary belongs to the Northern Metropolitan Region. Mitchell Shire identifies Beveridge and Wallan as the townships within that boundary, so they are the only Mitchell localities included here.

The 534-record inventory is a service-planning boundary, not a statement that every address can receive the same price, arrival window or installer. Remote Yarra Ranges, Cardinia and Mornington Peninsula localities remain in the data with lower service tiers.

## Priority model

| Tier | Records | Website treatment | Operational meaning |
|---|---:|---|---|
| 1 | 74 | Launch page candidate; index only after content and coverage QA | Strong launch hub or high-value city/growth-centre market |
| 2 | 276 | Staged page queue; no mass publish | Established urban coverage or important growth area, subject to installer capacity |
| 3 | 137 | Service finder / zone coverage list | Outer, hills, peninsula or rural-edge area; scheduled route or travel quote |
| 4 | 47 | Quote-only, restricted or no public page | Remote, very low-density, institutional, airport, defence or primarily non-residential locality |

Priority is a launch decision, not a claim about household income or individual job value. Straight-line CBD distance is used only as a consistent planning input. It must never be shown as a travel time.

## Installer dispatch rules to adopt before ads

1. **Record the installer home base and accepted zones.** Re-score travel after the first Melbourne installer is chosen. CBD distance is neutral for research, but the installer’s actual start point determines profitable coverage.
2. **Do not mix opposite sides of Melbourne in one run.** A west-to-south-east or north-to-peninsula transfer can consume the margin from a standard installation.
3. **Use zone days for outer work.** Peninsula, Yarra Ranges, Pakenham/Cardinia, Melton and Wallan work should be grouped until lead volume supports local installers.
4. **Collect access details before assignment.** The booking form or call script should ask for dwelling type, floor/lift, parking, stairs, wall material if known, TV size, bracket status, cable concealment request, fireplace/cabinetry and a wall photo.
5. **Use live pins for new estates and rural-edge properties.** Street names and navigation data can lag construction, while long driveways may not be visible from the road.
6. **Make electrical scope explicit.** A wall-mounting booking must not imply that unlicensed installers will add, move or alter fixed power. Route power work to an appropriately licensed electrical contractor.
7. **Set a travel-pricing rule before taking remote bookings.** The site should say “travel pricing confirmed before booking” for Tier 3 and Tier 4 areas until exact thresholds are approved.
8. **Keep a backup installer by side of city.** The minimum useful coverage matrix is west/north-west, north/east and south-east/peninsula; a single installer is a launch constraint, not citywide capacity.

## Page-generation data contract

Each JSON and CSV record contains:

- `name`, `official_name`, `slug`: display and routing fields;
- `lga_key`, `lga`, `planning_region`, `zone`: government and operational geography;
- `priority`, `page_status`, `index_recommendation`: rollout controls;
- `latitude`, `longitude`, `estimated_cbd_distance_km`: planning coordinates, not customer-facing travel promises;
- `nearby_areas`: the three closest official localities in the same operational zone;
- `regional_anchors`: broader local corridors, activity centres or physical features;
- `property_profile`: a dispatch-oriented property category;
- `local_context`: a concise locality seed for page drafting;
- `service_notes`: access and scheduling guidance.

`nearby_areas` is a straight-line geographic relationship, not a road-time calculation. It is useful for internal links and route grouping, but it should be sanity-checked before it appears in public copy.

## Page assembly pattern

A generated suburb page should have the same service offer and conversion design as the Brisbane site, but the body copy must be genuinely Melbourne-specific.

Recommended page structure:

1. **H1:** “TV wall mounting in {name}”.
2. **Opening:** the actual services offered, the coverage status and how quotes work.
3. **Local fit:** use `property_profile`, `nearby_areas` and `regional_anchors` to explain relevant access or wall-mounting considerations. Rewrite the seed into natural copy; do not print it unchanged across hundreds of pages.
4. **Job-scope block:** TV size, wall type, bracket, cable concealment, soundbar, fireplace or cabinetry, power and access.
5. **Coverage block:** nearby areas and the parent zone page.
6. **Trust block:** only claims that are true for the assigned installer, such as insurance, workmanship warranty or licence details.
7. **FAQ:** access, wall material, bracket supply, cable concealment and travel pricing for that tier.
8. **Quote form:** carries `suburb`, `zone`, landing-page slug and campaign attribution into the Melbourne lead sheet.

## Local SEO guardrails

- Do not create a fake Melbourne office, fake suburb address or “local team based in {suburb}” claim.
- Do not imply an installer has worked in a suburb until job records support that statement.
- Do not use a landmark merely to stuff a place name. It must help explain the catchment, access or nearby coverage.
- Do not promise arrival times from straight-line distance.
- Do not index Tier 2 pages in bulk. Human-review each batch for coverage truth, useful local detail, internal links and duplicated wording.
- Do not index Tier 3 or Tier 4 pages by default. A customer can still select those locations in a service finder and request a travel quote.
- Do not call a restricted, airport, defence or mainly industrial locality a normal residential suburb.
- Do not publish a page if the assigned installer will not accept jobs there.
- Keep each page’s structured data consistent with the real business address and service-area model; do not invent suburb-level branches.

## Recommended rollout sequence

### Before deployment

- Confirm the Melbourne call handler’s hours and escalation process.
- Appoint at least one vetted installer and record accepted zones, standard rate, large-TV surcharge, wall-type exclusions, cable-concealment scope, insurance and warranty.
- Set the Tier 3/4 travel-pricing rule.
- Make the Melbourne form and call source write only to the Melbourne spreadsheet.

### Initial website release

- Homepage and core service pages.
- Nine zone pages.
- Tier 1 pages in two or three batches, beginning with the zones the first installer will actually cover.
- Tier 2/3/4 service finder, with no automatic indexed page for lower tiers.

### After real leads arrive

- Add installer capacity by side of city.
- Review quote rate, booked-job rate, average travel time and margin by zone.
- Promote Tier 2 suburbs in batches where enquiries and capacity justify a useful page.
- Rewrite page context using real, non-identifying job patterns rather than generic location prose.

## Authoritative sources and method

1. [Vicmap Admin – Locality Polygon Aligned to Property](https://discover.data.vic.gov.au/dataset/vicmap-admin-locality-polygon-aligned-to-property) supplies the authoritative bounded-locality names and geometries. The catalogue says local governments define the boundaries and the Registrar of Geographic Names registers them. The dataset is weekly and was shown as updated 18 July 2026 when this research was completed.
2. [Victoria in Future – metropolitan regions](https://www.planning.vic.gov.au/guides-and-resources/Data-spatial-and-insights/discover-and-access-planning-open-data/victoria-in-future) supplies the six metropolitan regions and their council membership. It explicitly treats only the Urban Growth Boundary portion of Mitchell Shire as metropolitan.
3. [Melbourne’s future planning framework](https://www.planning.vic.gov.au/guides-and-resources/strategies-and-initiatives/melbournes-future-planning-framework) supplies the official regional descriptions, including the western growth areas, the established and well-treed east, the northern growth corridor, and the southern region extending from middle suburbs to the Mornington Peninsula and Bunyip.
4. [Greater Melbourne and urban zones](https://www.sro.vic.gov.au/owning-property/land-tax/exemptions-and-concessions-land-tax/primary-production-land/greater-melbourne-and-urban-zones) confirms that the Urban Growth Boundary is narrower than whole-council metropolitan planning areas and distinguishes complete from partial councils.
5. [Mitchell Shire Health and Human Services Gap Analysis 2026](https://www.mitchellshire.vic.gov.au/assets/general-downloads/MSC_Health-and-Human-Services-Gap-Analysis-Report_2026_web.pdf) identifies Beveridge and Wallan as the southern Mitchell townships inside Melbourne’s Urban Growth Boundary.
6. [Transport Victoria’s map of managed roads](https://transport.vic.gov.au/road-and-active-transport/business-and-industry/road-and-traffic-management/map-of-dtp-managed-roads) confirms the role of freeways and arterial roads as the principal links between metropolitan activity centres. The zone model uses those corridors as dispatch spines but does not calculate live travel times.
7. [Transport Victoria metropolitan public transport maps](https://transport.vic.gov.au/plan-a-journey/network-maps/melbourne-public-transport-maps) and local-area maps were used as a secondary check on metropolitan corridor names and activity-centre relationships.

Housing and access descriptions in the dataset are operational hypotheses at LGA or zone level, not claims about every property. Validate and refine them from wall photos, installer notes and completed Melbourne jobs.
