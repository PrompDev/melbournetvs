## Start with the TV, wall and access

A safe mounting plan depends on more than screen size. We review the TV model or weight, the bracket you have or need, wall construction, nearby outlets, furniture, apartment access and whether a two-person lift is required.

Send a front-on wall photo, a wider room photo and the TV make and size. If you already have a bracket, include its label or packaging. That gives the assigned installer enough information to confirm the likely method and quote the agreed scope.

## What standard mounting can include

Standard work can include bracket positioning, suitable mechanical fixings, levelling, connection to existing outlets and a basic function check. Cable management, soundbar mounting, difficult access and very large screens are itemised when relevant.

New or relocated fixed power is not bundled into ordinary mounting. It requires a Victorian licensed electrician. Regulated communications cabling requires an appropriately registered cabler.

The service is opening zone by zone as primary and backup installers are verified. Availability is checked against the address before any appointment is promised.
