Melbourne Wall Types

TV Mounting for Every
Melbourne Wall Type.

Melbourne homes include brick, concrete, plasterboard, timber, AAC and specialist walls. The substrate and fixing method are confirmed before drilling.

Brick

HEPA dust extraction, brick-face drilling, rated dynabolts — never plastic plugs.

Concrete

SDS-plus tooling with sleeve anchors rated well beyond the TV's load.

Gyprock

Asbestos-tested pre-1987, stud-caught where possible, Snaptoggle on cavity runs.

Timber Stud

Pilot-drilled dead-centre. Structural #14 lag screws, pull-tested every fixing.

VJ & Weatherboard

Heritage-safe fixings. Cables routed internal. Zero visible damage.

Hebel / AAC

Dedicated Hebel sleeves, torqued to spec. Standard anchors crumble this stuff.

Double Brick

Cavity-depth scoped before drilling. Anchors that bite the inner skin.

How to Hide TV Cables In a Melbourne period timber home — Without Cutting Timber ›

Hiding TV cables in a Melbourne period timber home is nothing like mounting on gyprock. VJ boards are 30mm of solid hardwood. Behind them sits a picture rail at about 1.2 metres — enough for two screws, not a full bracket. Drill it wrong and you split century-old timber. Most DIY guides skip this bit.

We run cables the way Melbourne sparkies have for decades. On high-set period timber homes we drop through the floor and come up where you want power, HDMI and surround. On low-sets and flats we fish through the wall cavity into a recessed GPO behind the TV. Confirmed wall mount installs across Carlton, Brunswick, Northcote, Fitzroy and Port Melbourne. No split timber. No visible cables. Bracket included, written scope.
